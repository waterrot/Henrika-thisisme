1. Swimming degree

Hendrika's first accomplishment might just be her best. When she was 6 years old she learned to swim and soon turned out to be quite the best in her class. One day, she saved 4 classmates from drowning (of which one was a mysterious cat). She became a lifeguard at 8 to prevent beachgoers to drown in the raging waves of the North Sea. She also saved 12 bugs from drowning in a swimming pool while camping in France. Legend has that she used to be a blobfish in her former life, which might explain this particular talent of hers.

2. Middle School

At the age of 12, Hendrika was send to the GSG Guido in Amersfoort, one of the best schools in the Netherlands. There, she had the opportunity to develop her many talents into a large range of masterskills which she now possesses so proudly. She's also admired for her biking skills, as she had to bike for over two hours to school and back each day. For this, King William-Alexander of Orange-Nassau personally awarded her in the Order of the Royal Military Bicycle Lions of Honours, the highest reward for biking in the Netherlands. She almost graduated magna cum laude in 2018, except for a couple of courses that she perfected at Luzac College later. As a tribute, she was given the key to the school. A room was named after her where magnificent frescoes depicting her famous deeds can still be seen, a cultural tour de force.

3. Rowing

When you're good - no, great - at many things, those things might become boring easily. Exactly this happened to Hendrika in sports. After mastering the arts of golf, soccer, skydiving, surfing, the Tibetan martial arts of Tescao, chess (a sport for the mind), and tennis on the Nintendo Wii-console, she started her career in the revered sport of rowing at the Hilversum rowing association Cornelis Tromp - the best in the country. After her very first strokes, even here she turned out to be the greatest. She became a household name. However, het advances became quite the problem as no other rowers or teams could catch up to her. So she went on, first to the Dutch National finals, then the European Championships, and several World Cups after that. She won all of them. It is said that legendary rower Roel Braas ate his shoe after she beat him on the 2k trail.

4. 18 years old

When Hendrika became 18 years old, she (finally) was allowed to drink in the Netherlands. A huge party was thrown in her honours, with several celebrities, famous business people, and politicians attending the birthday ceremony - the one where the candles on the cake are blown out by the birthday girl. She got them all in one blow. Moreover, she downed five bottles of Smirnoff Ice without puking (or so we think), for which she received the lifelong reward of free Smirnoff Booze at Albert Heijn, a high-end luxury warehouse company. This new age became turning point in her magnificent life. She attended Windesheim College in Zwolle to study Industrial Design. She designed several famous buildings, such as the colosseum in Rome, the Eiffel Tower in Paris, the first Rubik's Cube, and several of the pyramids near Giza, Egypt. In fact, it is speculated that the Egyptian Sphinx itself was made in her image.
